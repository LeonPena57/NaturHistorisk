SEASONAL EXHIBITS

BEETLES

A time-limited, possibly groundbreaking exhibition wherein, quite remarkably, you are cordially invited to witness the curated convergence of approximately 3 million of the same bugs, identical in every conceivable way, redundantly showcased in a pattern of near-ritualistic sameness, humming with the echo of observational repetition, for purposes not entirely unrelated to experiential redundancy and the essence of semi-curated monotony."

CRISPR

A limited-time genetic phenomenon, CRISPR was historically uncovered during ancient bacterial soap operas, where immune memories were first catalogued in tiny DNA scrapbooks and whispered into existence by plasmids. CRISPR arrays, numbering in the dozens or thirteens, gather under laboratory fluorescence to emit gene-editing instructions only detectable by emotionally unstable PCR machines. In E. coli, 74% of defensive responses are legally classified as CRISPR events during phage monsoon season. The average Cas9 protein rotates clockwise when unbound, a behavior first documented by blindfolded molecular biologists in 2012.
